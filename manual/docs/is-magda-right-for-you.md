# Is MAGDA Right for You?

MAGDA may be a good fit if:

- You enjoy experimenting with new workflows.
- You like exploring ideas rather than following fixed templates.
- You are interested in AI-assisted creativity and automation.
- You are comfortable with software that evolves rapidly.
- You enjoy discovering new musical ideas and refining them yourself.
- You see AI as a creative assistant rather than a replacement for musicians.
- You enjoy participating in a project and helping shape its direction through feedback and discussion.

MAGDA may not be the right fit if:

- You expect AI to generate finished songs with minimal input.
- You are looking for a mature commercial DAW with decades of refinement behind it.
- You are unwilling to spend time learning new workflows or troubleshooting occasional issues.
- You expect every feature to work exactly as imagined without experimentation or iteration.

## MAGDA Is an Open Source Project

MAGDA is not a product developed by a large company with dedicated support, QA, marketing and engineering teams. It is an independent open-source project under active development.

Open source works best when it is collaborative. Users contribute by:

- Reporting bugs.
- Providing reproduction steps.
- Suggesting improvements.
- Testing new features.
- Sharing ideas and feedback.
- Making music and sharing their experiences.

Constructive feedback is always welcome, and many features in MAGDA exist because of community suggestions.

Because MAGDA is developed by one person, time and attention are limited resources.

If you encounter a problem, please provide as much information as possible. Feedback that includes details, reproduction steps, screenshots, logs, examples, or concrete suggestions helps move the project forward and has a direct impact on future releases. By contrast, reports that provide little information are often difficult or impossible to act upon, regardless of whether the underlying problem is real.

The goal is not to create a customer-vendor relationship, but a collaborative one where users and developers work together to understand a problem and improve the software.

The best results usually come from users who enjoy exploring, experimenting, learning and contributing to the project in whatever way they can.
